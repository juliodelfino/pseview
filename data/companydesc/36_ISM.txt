ISM Communications Corporation (ISM) was originally a mining company incorporated in March 1925, under the name Itogon-Suyoc Mines, Inc. In the early 2000s, ISM was transformed to a company engaged in information technology, multimedia telecommunications, and other similar industries. This business was engaged by the Company thru Eastern Telecommunications Philippines, Inc. (ETPI).  In 2010, ISM sold 100% of its interest in A.G.N. Philippines, Inc., the corporate vehicle through which the Company held 40% interest in ETPI, to Vega Telecom, Inc. (VTI). On December 29, 2010, the Company and VTI executed the sale documents for this transaction and consequently reduced the Company&#39;s interest in ETPI to 37.7%. On June 28, 2011, the Executive Committee authorized the Company to divest its interest in ETPI and sell it to San Miguel Equity Securities, Inc.<br/><br/>In 2010, ISM and PhilWeb Corporation (WEB) purchased a 65% stake in German information technology firm, Acentic GmbH (Acentic). Following this, WEB and ISM are set to launch Acentic Asia, a subsidiary of Acentic, which is envisioned to serve as Acentic&#39;s gateway to Asia. In the Philippines, Acentic&#39;s business is being done under Acentic Philippines, Inc. Acentic offers digital and Internet protocol converged services to hotels, tourism outfits and healthcare facilities.<br/><br/>In 2011, the Company, together with other investors (the ISM Group), purchased a total of 47,909,473 common shares and 120,000,000 preferred shares or a total of 167,909,473 voting shares of the Philippine Bank of Communications (PBC). This includes the Company&#39;s share of approximately 37.11% of the bank. The ISM Group obtained all the necessary regulatory approvals on December 22, 2011. On August 15, 2014, the Board of ISM accepted the offer of Mr. Lucio L. Co, through his delegated entity, VFC Land Resources, Inc. (VFC), to purchase ISM&#39;s 59,241,438 common shares of PBC. The sale agreement with VFC was signed on September 29, 2014. As a result of the sale, the Company now owns 4,806,987 common shares of PBC, which is equivalent to 1.00% of PBC&#39;s outstanding capital stock.<br/><br/>As of December 31, 2015, ISM has no operations, other than that of a holding company.<br/><br/>Source: SEC Form 17-A (2015) & The Company (2015)
