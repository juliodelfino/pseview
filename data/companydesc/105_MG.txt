Millennium Global Holdings, Inc. (MG), formerly IPVG Corporation (IPVG), was incorporated on May 19, 1964 primarily to invest and engage in management of investments in media technology industry: internet, intranet, extranet, and all types of information technology users; purchase, sale, import and export, license, distribution and rental of any computer hardware and equipment; and engage in e-commerce.<br/><br/>On July 1, 2011, pursuant to a corporate restructuring, the shareholders of the Company approved the sale of all assets and liabilities of IPVG to a new private company, IP Ventures, Inc. (IPVI), having the exact same shareholding structure of IPVG. Pursuant thereto, MG transferred substantially all of its assets to IPVI. On February 14, 2013, the Securities and Exchange Commission approved the change in corporate name from IPVG to the present one, as well as the change in primary purpose to a general holding company.<br/><br/>On December 20, 2013, the stockholders approved the investment in and/or acquisition of various businesses and operating companies, including 51% controlling interest in Millennium Ocean Star Corporation (MOSC). By the following month, the Company acquired 51% controlling interest in MOSC.<br/><br/>On October 14, 2014, the Board of Directors of MG approved the acquisition of Cebu Canning Corporation (C3) through a combined acquisition of primary and secondary shares, making it a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Company. On April 17, 2015, C3 became a subsidiary (51%) of MG.<br/><br/>Source: SEC Form 17-A (2015)<br/>
