Harbor Star Shipping Services, Inc. (TUGS) was registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on July 5, 1988 under the name Seatows, Inc. The Company&#39;s primary purpose is to engage in ocean towage, commerce and navigation in the carriage of goods and passengers by water upon the oceans, seas, sounds, lakes, rivers, canals, bays, harbors, and other waterways between the various ports of the Philippines and between such ports and other ports in the world.<br/><br/>The Company&#39;s maritime services include harbor assistance; lighterage; towing; salvage; marine repair and maintenance works; and other marine services. On August 11, 2015, TUGS secured the approval of the SEC to expand its business scope to include international towage, commerce and navigation in the carriage of goods and passengers, and international manning, recruitment, contracting and ship and crew management for all types of vessels.<br/><br/>On September 17, 2015, TUGS incorporated a wholly-owned subsidiary, Harbor Star Subic Corporation, which shall offer marine-related ancillary services such as harbor assistance, towage, lighterage, oil-spill response and underwater marine services to vessels and terminals operating within the Subic Bay Freeport Zone.<br/><br/>TUGS also owns a 45% stake in Peak Flag Sdn. Bhd. (Peak Flag), a company incorporated in Malaysia to provide marine services at the North and Kuantan Ports. Peak Flag commenced operations in February 2015.<br/><br/>As of December 31, 2015, the Company has established operations in 14 base ports all over the country, providing services to approximately 6,164 ship calls. The major ports that the Company services include the Manila International Container Terminal, Bataan, Batangas, Cagayan de Oro, and Davao.<br/><br/>Source: SEC Form 17-A (2015)<br/>
